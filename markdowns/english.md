### Word Classes (Classes de palavras)

- **Noun (Substantivo)**: Names people, places, things, feelings, and ideas.  
  **Examples**: Gustavo, dog, car, the FBI, flower.

- **Adjective (Adjetivo)**: Qualifies or characterizes a noun.  
  **Examples**: beautiful, fast, happy, sad.

- **Verb (Verbo)**: Indicates an action, a state, or a natural phenomenon.  
  **Examples**: run, see, play, watch, study.

- **Adverb (Advérbio)**: Modifies a verb, an adjective, or another adverb.  
  Indicates manner, intensity, time, frequency, or place.  
  **Examples**: quickly (rapidamente), bravely (bravamente), happily (felizmente), why (por que), how (como).

- **Pronoun (Pronome)**: Replaces a noun.  
  **Examples**: you, my, myself (eu mesmo), this, who (quem), nobody (ninguém).

- **Prepositions (Preposições)**: Link words, indicating relationships of time, place, direction, cause, etc.  
  **Examples**: to (para), of, in, by, with.

- **Conjunctions (Conjunções)**: Link words, phrases, or clauses, showing logical relationships between them.  
  **Examples**: but, because, and, although (embora), or.

- **Articles (Artigos)**: Define or limit a noun.  
  **Examples**: the, a (um/uma), an (um/uma, antes de vogal).

- **Interjections (Interjeições)**: Express emotions or sudden reactions.  
  **Examples**: ouch!, wow!, hey!

> In English, adjectives usually come **before** the noun!  
> Example: red apple, beautiful house, a strange noise.

### Word Order (SVO)

- **Affirmative sentences**:
  - **Basic structure**:  
    `Subject + Verb (Conjugated) + Object/Complement (optional)`
  - **Examples**:
    - ***I play soccer.*** = Eu jogo futebol.  
      Verbal form: First person singular, simple present.
    - ***You wanted to play a game.*** = Você queria jogar um jogo.  
      Verbal form: Second person singular, simple past, infinitive (to play).
    - ***They will read a book.*** = Eles irão ler um livro.  
      Verbal form: Third person plural, simple future.

- **Negative sentences**:
  - **Basic structure**:  
    `Subject + Auxiliary verb + Negation (not) + Main verb + Object/Complement`  
    > In negative statements, the auxiliary verb is usually contracted.  
    > Examples: ***is not*** → **isn't**, ***do not*** → **don't**, ***will not*** → **won't**
    
  - **Examples**:
    - ***I don't play soccer.*** = Eu não jogo futebol.  
      Verbal form: First person singular, simple present.
    - ***You didn't want to play a game.*** = Você não queria jogar um jogo.  
      Verbal form: Second person singular, simple past.
    - ***They won't read a book.*** = Eles não irão ler um livro.  
      Verbal form: Third person plural, simple future.

- **Interrogative sentences**:
  - **Basic structure**:  
    `Auxiliary verb or verb to be + Subject + Main verb + Object/Complement`
    
  - **Examples**:
    - ***Do I play soccer well?*** = Eu jogo futebol bem?  
      Verbal form: First person singular, simple present.
    - ***Did you know how to play football?*** = Você sabia como jogar futebol?  
      Verbal form: Second person singular, simple past.
    - ***Will he play football?*** = Ele jogará futebol?  
      Verbal form: Third person singular, simple future.

### Verbs

#### Regular and Irregular Verbs

**Regular verbs** are verbs that follow a fixed pattern to indicate tense or mood.
Usually, they add the suffix **-ed** in the past tense and past participle.  
**Examples**: worked, played, opened.  
> Note: All verbs (work, play, open) add **-ed** to indicate the past tense.

**Irregular verbs**, however, do not follow this pattern.  
They have their own forms for the past simple and past participle.  
**Examples**:

| Base form (Simple Present) | Past Simple   | Past Participle |
|----------------------------|---------------|-----------------|
| Go                         | Went (foi)    | Gone (ido)      |
| Eat                        | Ate (comeu)   | Eaten (comido)  |
| See                        | Saw (viu)     | Seen (visto)    |
| Have                       | Had (teve)    | Had (tido)      |

#### Verb Tenses

| Tense Name                     | Structure                           | Example                                | Tradução                                 |
|--------------------------------|-------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| **Simple Present**             | Base verb (+s/es for he/she/it)     | She works every day.                   | Ela trabalha todos os dias.              |
| **Simple Past**                | Base verb + -ed (regular verbs)     | We visited Paris.                      | Nós visitamos Paris.                     |
| **Present Continuous**         | am/is/are + verb(-ing)              | I am studying now.                     | Eu estou estudando agora.                |
| **Present Perfect**            | have/has + past participle          | They have finished the task.           | Eles terminaram a tarefa.                |
| **Present Perfect Continuous** | have/has been + verb(-ing)          | He has been working all day.           | Ele está trabalhando o dia todo.         |
| **Past Continuous**            | was/were + verb(-ing)               | She was reading a book.                | Ela estava lendo um livro.               |
| **Past Perfect**               | had + past participle               | I had eaten before you arrived.        | Eu tinha comido antes de você chegar.    |
| **Past Perfect Continuous**    | had been + verb(-ing)               | They had been studying for hours.      | Eles estavam estudando há horas.         |
| **Simple Future**              | will + base verb                    | I will call you tomorrow.              | Eu vou te ligar amanhã.                  |
| **Future Perfect**             | will have + past participle         | He will have finished by then.         | Ele terá terminado até lá.               |
| **Future Continuous**          | will be + verb(-ing)                | She will be traveling at 9 AM.         | Ela estará viajando às 9h.               |
| **Future Perfect Continuous**  | will have been + verb(-ing)         | We will have been working for 5 hours. | Estaremos trabalhando há 5 horas.        |

#### Verbs to/be

| Form  | Tempo e tipo do Verbo  | Pessoa(s) usada(s)    | Exemplo                | Tradução                                                                 |
|-------|-------------------------|-----------------------|------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| am    | Simple Present          | I                     | I am happy             | Eu estou feliz. (am == sou/estou)                                        |
| is    | Simple Present          | He, She, It           | She is at home         | Ela está em casa. (is == é/está)                                         |
| are   | Simple Present          | You, We, They         | They are students      | Eles são estudantes. (are == são/estão)                                  |
| was   | Simple Past             | I, He, She, It        | He was tired           | Ele estava cansado. (was == era/estava)                                  |
| were  | Simple Past             | You, We, They         | We were late           | Nós estávamos atrasados. (were == eram/estavam)                          |
| be    | Base form / Infinitive  | Usado com auxiliares  | I want to be a teacher | Eu quero ser professor. (be == ser/estar)                                |
| been  | Past Participle         | Com have/has/had      | She has been sick      | Ela tem estado doente. (been == sido/estado)                             |
| being | Gerúndio                | Ação contínua/passiva | He is being nice today | Ele está sendo legal hoje. (being == sendo)                              |
